Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is pet fostering?

    Pet fostering is a temporary arrangement where individuals or families care for pets in their homes until they are adopted. It helps animals adjust to home life and reduces overcrowding in shelters.

  • How do I become a foster pet parent?

    To become a foster pet parent, fill out our foster application form on the website. After a review, we will contact you for an interview and provide necessary training.

  • What are the responsibilities of a foster caregiver?

    As a foster caregiver, you will provide daily care, including feeding, grooming, training, and socializing the pet. You will also be responsible for taking the pet to veterinary appointments if needed.

  • How long does a typical foster period last?

    The length of the foster period can vary from a few weeks to several months, depending on the pet's needs and the adoption process. We will keep you informed about the status of your foster pet.

  • Can I adopt the pet I foster?

    Yes! If you fall in love with your foster pet, you have the option to adopt them. We‚Äôll guide you through the adoption process.

  • What supplies will I need for fostering?

    We provide basic supplies like food, litter, and toys. You may need to provide a comfortable space for the pet, such as a bed, and any additional toys or supplies you feel are necessary.

  • What if I have pets already at home?

    It's great if you have pets! We recommend introducing them slowly and ensuring they are comfortable with each other. Our team can provide guidance on how to manage introductions.

  • How can I help if I cannot foster a pet?

    If you cannot foster, you can help by volunteering, donating supplies, or financially supporting our organization. Every bit helps us care for pets in need!
